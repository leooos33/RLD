# Core Concepts

Before trading on RLD, it helps to understand a few key ideas that make the protocol work. This page covers the essentials — no code, no formulas, just how things fit together.

---

## What Are Interest Rate Derivatives?

In traditional finance, interest rates are traded as standalone instruments — bond futures, interest rate swaps, swaptions. Trillion-dollar markets exist for managing rate exposure. In DeFi, this market has been almost entirely missing.

RLD brings interest rate trading on-chain. Instead of trading the *price* of an asset (like ETH or BTC), you trade the *borrowing rate* of a lending pool (like Aave USDC or Morpho USDT).

**Why would you trade rates?**
- **Hedging:** If you're borrowing on Aave at a floating rate, a rate spike could blow up your P&L. Going long on RLD protects you.
- **Speculation:** DeFi rates are extremely volatile — they can 10× during leverage spikes. This creates outsized profit opportunities.
- **Fixed income:** Convert unpredictable floating yields into locked, predictable fixed returns.

---

## The Index Price

Every RLD market tracks a specific lending pool's borrowing rate and converts it into a dollar-denominated price:

```text
Index Price = 100 × Borrow Rate
```

| Borrow Rate | Index Price |
| --- | --- |
| 3% (0.03) | $3.00 |
| 5% (0.05) | $5.00 |
| 12% (0.12) | $12.00 |
| 50% (0.50) | $50.00 |

If Aave USDC borrow rate doubles from 5% to 10%, the index price doubles from \$5 to \$10. Your position's value moves proportionally.

---

## The Mark Price

The **mark price** is the actual trading price of the wRLP token on the Uniswap V4 pool. In a healthy market, the mark price tracks the index price closely. When they diverge, the funding mechanism pushes them back together.

```text
Mark Price  = what wRLP is trading at on the pool
Index Price = what the underlying rate says it should be worth
```

## Funding Rate (How Mark Tracks Index)

RLD uses a continuous funding mechanism — not discrete funding payments like traditional perp DEXs. Instead of periodic cash transfers between longs and shorts, RLD adjusts a global **Normalization Factor** that continuously shifts value:

- **When mark > index (premium):** Long holders slowly decay, short holders benefit. This incentivizes selling to push the mark price down.
- **When mark < index (discount):** Short holders' debt grows, long holders benefit. This incentivizes buying to push the mark price up.

The effect is automatic and continuous — no manual claiming of funding, no snapshots. The price of wRLP adjusts in real-time to reflect accumulated funding.

> **For traders:** Funding is invisible in your token balance. It's embedded in the price. A long position in a premium market will slowly lose value even if the index stays flat — this is the "cost of carry."

## Margin Accounts (Brokers)

Every user on RLD trades through a **margin account** — an isolated smart contract that holds your collateral and tracks your positions.

**Key properties:**
- **Isolated:** Each account is independent. One account's liquidation doesn't affect another.
- **NFT-owned:** Your account is controlled by an ERC-721 NFT. Whoever holds the NFT controls the account.
- **Cross-margined internally:** Within one account, your collateral, LP positions, and TWAMM orders all count toward your margin.
- **Delegatable:** You can grant trading access to up to 8 operator addresses (bots, vaults, other contracts) without giving up ownership.

**What counts as margin:**

| Asset | Counted in NAV? |
| --- | --- |
| waUSDC balance (cash) | ✅ Yes |
| wRLP balance (position tokens) | ✅ Yes, at index price |
| Active V4 LP position (tracked) | ✅ Yes |
| Active TWAMM order (tracked) | ✅ Yes |
| Limit orders | ❌ No — invisible to margin |
| Untracked LP positions | ❌ No — invisible to margin |

### Cross-Margin Example

Because RLD natively cross-margins everything inside a broker, you can use yield-generating assets to back your trades. 

**Example Scenario:**
1. You deposit **$10,000 USDC** into your margin account.
2. You provide all of it as liquidity to the wRLP/waUSDC pool.
3. Your account now holds **an LP position worth ~$10,000**.
4. Even though you have no "cash" left, your LP position counts as collateral. 
5. You can now mint and sell (short) **$5,000 of wRLP debt**, using the LP position itself to satisfy the 120% initial margin requirement.

This allows you to earn LP fees and Aave yield simultaneously while running a directional short, vastly increasing capital efficiency.

## Collateralization & Health Factor

Your margin account must always hold enough value to back your debt. This is measured by the **health factor:**

```text
Health Factor = Net Worth / Total Debt
```

### Calculating Net Worth

Net Worth is the total value of all assets held inside your margin account. Because RLD is a cross-margin system, everything your account holds contributes to your net worth:

```text
Net Worth = 
    + waUSDC balance (Cash)
    + wRLP balance × Index Price (Long Positions)
    + Active V4 LP position value
    + Active TWAMM order value
```

**Why is wRLP tracked as collateral?**
Unlike traditional perps where your "position" is just an abstract number in a database, on RLD your long position is a real ERC-20 token (`wRLP`). If you buy `wRLP` tokens, they sit in your margin account. 

To determine your account's health, the protocol must value those tokens. Crucially, the protocol always values your `wRLP` holdings using the **Index Price** — not the Uniswap pool's market price. This protects your account from being liquidated due to short-term market manipulation or flash crashes on the DEX.

- **Opening a position:** Your health factor must be above the **initial margin** (e.g., 120%).
- **Staying safe:** Your health factor must stay above the **maintenance margin** (e.g., 109%).
- **Getting liquidated:** If your health factor drops below maintenance, anyone can liquidate your account.

**What moves your health factor:**
- ⬇️ Rates rising (if you're short, your debt grows, lowering health)
- ⬇️ Rates falling (if you're long, your position loses value, lowering health)
- ⬇️ Funding decay (holding a position in a premium/discount market decreases net worth)
- ⬆️ Adding collateral (increases net worth)
- ⬆️ LP fees accruing (increases net worth)
- ⬆️ Aave yield on your waUSDC (increases net worth)

## The wRLP Token

**wRLP** is the position token of the protocol. It's a standard ERC-20 that represents exposure to the interest rate:

- **Holding wRLP = Long exposure.** If rates go up, wRLP becomes more valuable.
- **Owing wRLP (debt) = Short exposure.** If rates go down, your debt decreases and you profit.

wRLP trades freely on the Uniswap V4 pool. You can buy it (go long), mint it against collateral (go short), or provide liquidity with it.

## TWAMM (Time-Weighted Average Market Making)

Instead of executing a large swap instantly, TWAMM distributes your order evenly over a chosen time period. This gives you a time-weighted average price, avoiding front-running and reducing slippage.

**Example:** Instead of selling 100,000 wRLP in a single transaction, submit a 24-hour TWAMM order that drips out ~4,166 wRLP per hour. Your average execution price will smooth out intra-day volatility.

TWAMM is used automatically by synthetic bonds for their buy-back schedules, but you can also submit manual TWAMM orders from the trading interface.
