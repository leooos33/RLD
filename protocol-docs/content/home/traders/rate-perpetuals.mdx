# Rate Perpetuals

Rate-Level Perpetuals (RLP) transform DeFi's most volatile but overlooked metric — the lending pool borrowing rate — into a tradable, persistent derivative. Instead of trying to predict ETH price, you're trading whether Aave USDC borrow rates will go up or down.

---

## Why Trade Rates?

DeFi borrow rates have a unique payoff profile that doesn't exist anywhere else in crypto:

**Structurally bounded, violently volatile.**

- **The floor:** Rates can't go below 0% and practically stay above ~3-4% (the risk-free rate floor set by SOFR / T-Bills). If DeFi rates drop below T-Bill yields, institutional capital arbitrages the gap.
- **The ceiling:** Rates are hard-capped by the lending pool's maximum (~75-100% APY on Aave's Slope 2 curve).
- **The spikes:** Between floor and ceiling, rates can 10-20× during leverage surges, liquidation cascades, or airdrop farming events.

This creates a **mean-reverting, positively skewed instrument**. This is fundamentally different from trading ETH (which can trend infinitely in either direction).

---

## Going Long (Buying wRLP)

**You believe rates will go up.** You spend collateral to buy wRLP tokens on the pool.

**When to go long:**
- ETH rally incoming → leveraged long demand → utilization and rates rise.
- Market panic → liquidation cascades → utilization maxes out → rates explode.
- Hedging your Aave borrow cost → lock in today's rate.


**How it works:**
1. From your funded margin account, click **Long**.
2. Enter the amount of waUSDC to spend.
3. The protocol swaps your waUSDC for wRLP via the Uniswap V4 pool.
4. Your wRLP tokens sit in your margin account.
5. If the index price rises (rates go up), your wRLP is worth more. Close anytime.

**P&L:**
```text
Profit = (Exit Price - Entry Price) × Quantity
```

> **Watch out for funding:** If mark > index, you pay continuous funding as a long. In a sustained premium, this erodes your position even if rates are flat.

---

## Going Short (Minting wRLP Debt)

**You believe rates will go down.** You mint wRLP tokens against your collateral and sell them. If rates drop, you buy them back cheaper and pocket the difference.

**When to go short:**
- Market cooling off after a rate spike → rates mean-reverting back to baseline.
- Creating a synthetic bond → short + TWAMM buy-back = fixed yield.
- Hedging your Aave lending yield → lock in today's rate for a custom maturity.


**How it works:**
1. From your funded margin account, click **Short**.
2. Enter your initial collateral and target debt amount.
3. The protocol mints wRLP, sells it on the pool, and deposits the proceeds back into your account.
4. Your debt is tracked at the current normalization factor.
5. If the index price falls (rates drop), your debt decreases in value. Close to realize profit.

**P&L:**
```text
Profit = (Entry Price - Exit Price) × Quantity - Funding Cost
```

**Leverage:** Because the sell proceeds are deposited back as collateral, shorts are inherently leveraged.

---

## Closing Positions

### Close Long
Sell your wRLP tokens back to the pool for waUSDC. The proceeds stay in your margin account.

### Close Short
Buy back wRLP from the pool to repay your debt. If the rate dropped, you buy back cheaper than you sold — that's your profit.

**Partial closes** are supported — you don't have to close everything at once. The protocol automatically caps debt repayment to your outstanding balance to prevent errors.

---

## The Asymmetric Edge

Interest rates have a property that asset prices don't: **guaranteed mean reversion**. When Aave USDC hits 50% APY during a leverage event:
- New suppliers flood in to capture high yield → supply increases.
- Existing borrowers repay expensive debt → demand decreases.
- Both forces mechanically push utilization (and rates) back to equilibrium.

This means that long positions in rate perps have a built-in convergence guarantee that doesn't exist in traditional crypto derivatives. The rate *will* come back. The question is timing and magnitude.
