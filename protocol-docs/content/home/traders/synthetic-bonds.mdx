# Synthetic Bonds

Synthetic bonds let you fix your yield or borrowing cost for any duration you choose — 1 week, 3 months, or 2 years. Unlike traditional DeFi fixed-rate protocols that fragment liquidity across discrete maturity dates, RLD routes all maturities through a single perpetual pool. No roll risk. No liquidity fragmentation. Permissionless exit.

---

## How It Works

A synthetic bond is a packaged strategy that combines three things:

1. **Deposit** — Your USDC goes into Aave, earning floating supply yield.
2. **Short** — The protocol mints wRLP and sells it, locking in today's rate as an upfront cash payment.
3. **TWAMM Buy-Back** — A streaming order slowly buys back the wRLP over your chosen duration, systematically unwinding the hedge.

The result: you receive a **fixed yield at entry**, regardless of what floating rates do afterward.

```text
                                        Your Bond
                        ┌─────────────────────────────────────────┐
                        │                                         │
  Deposit USDC ────────►│  waUSDC collateral earning Aave yield   │
                        │  + Short wRLP (locked-in rate)          │
                        │  + TWAMM buying back wRLP over time     │
                        │                                         │
                        │  At maturity: TWAMM finishes,           │
                        │  debt is repaid, collateral returned    │
                        └─────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

---

## Why It Works

**If rates fall:** Your Aave yield drops, but your short wRLP position gains value — the wRLP you need to buy back is now cheaper. The profit from the short offsets the lost yield.

**If rates rise:** Your Aave yield increases, compensating for the higher cost of buying back wRLP. The extra yield offsets the short position's loss.

In both scenarios, your net return converges to the rate you locked in at entry. The TWAMM streaming order ensures the unwind happens gradually and predictably, avoiding single-block execution risk.

## Creating a Bond

1. Navigate to the **Bonds** market.
2. Choose your **notional** (how much USDC to deposit).
3. Choose your **duration** (how long the fixed yield lasts).
4. Review the **fixed rate** — this is the yield you'll receive, locked at the moment of creation.
5. Confirm the transaction.

**Behind the scenes**, the BondFactory:
- Creates a new margin account.
- Deposits your USDC as collateral.
- Mints and sells wRLP to monetize the yield upfront.
- Submits a TWAMM streaming order to buy back the wRLP over your duration.
- **Freezes** the account — locking all positions in place for the duration.

The freeze ensures nobody (not even you) can tamper with the bond's positions until maturity. The TWAMM order continues executing automatically inside the protocol's engine.

---

## At Maturity

When the TWAMM order finishes:
1. **Unfreeze** the account.
2. **Claim** the TWAMM buy-back proceeds (the wRLP tokens bought over the duration).
3. **Repay** the remaining debt.
4. **Withdraw** your collateral + locked-in yield.

If rates moved against you during the bond, the hedge already absorbed the difference. Your net return should closely match the fixed rate you locked in at entry.

## Use Cases

### Fixed Yield on Stablecoins
You have $100k in USDC sitting idle. Instead of earning 5% floating on Aave (which could drop to 2% next week), you create a 6-month bond and lock in 5% guaranteed.

### Fixed-Cost Borrowing
You're running a delta-neutral basis trade and need to borrow at a predictable rate. Create a bond to fix your borrow cost so funding spikes don't compress your P&L.

### Yield Monetization
Cash out future yield today. The short-sell proceeds give you an upfront payment representing the present value of your locked rate over the chosen duration. Useful for treasury management or capital reallocation.

## Key Properties

| Property | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| **Duration** | Arbitrary — 1 hour to multi-year |
| **Collateral** | USDC → waUSDC (earns Aave yield while locked) |
| **Liquidity** | No fragmentation — all bonds use the same wRLP pool |
| **Transferable** | Yes — the bond is an NFT. Transfer it to sell your fixed-rate position |
| **Early exit** | Unfreeze early and close positions (forfeits the fixed-rate guarantee) |
| **Liquidation risk** | Yes — if rates spike extremely, the bond's margin account can still be liquidated |

> **Important:** Bonds are not risk-free. Extreme rate spikes can push the margin account toward liquidation. The initial over-collateralization (typically ~9% LTV) provides a large buffer, but not infinite protection.
