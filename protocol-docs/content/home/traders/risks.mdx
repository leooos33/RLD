# Risks

RLD is a derivatives protocol. Like all derivatives, positions carry risk. This page covers the key risks you should understand before trading.

---

## Liquidation

If your margin account's health factor drops below the maintenance margin (~109%), your account becomes eligible for liquidation.

**How liquidation works on RLD:**

RLD uses a **Dutch auction** — not instant liquidation. When your account becomes underwater:

1. A time-decay auction begins automatically.
2. The liquidation discount starts at 0% and increases over time.
3. Liquidators wait until the discount covers their costs, then step in.
4. The liquidator repays some or all of your debt and receives your collateral at a discount.

**Why Dutch auctions?** Instant liquidations in traditional DeFi cause cascading slippage — one big liquidation crashes the price, triggering more liquidations. The Dutch auction spreads liquidation over time, giving the market time to absorb the selling pressure.

**How to avoid liquidation:**
- Maintain a health factor well above 1.5×.
- Monitor your positions during volatile periods.
- Add collateral if your health factor drops.
- Don't over-leverage — the protocol allows high leverage, but that doesn't mean you should use it.

## Funding Decay

The normalization factor continuously adjusts based on the premium or discount between mark and index:

- **If you're long in a premium market** (mark > index): Your wRLP tokens slowly lose value relative to a static position. This is the cost of carry.
- **If you're short in a discount market** (mark < index): Your debt grows faster than the rate alone would imply.

Funding is not a fee — it's a value transfer between longs and shorts. But if you're on the wrong side for an extended period, it can significantly erode your position.

> **Tip:** Check the current funding rate before opening a position. A large premium or discount means you're paying for the privilege of holding your position.

## Smart Contract Risk

RLD is built on Ethereum with Uniswap V4 as the underlying pool. While the protocol undergoes rigorous auditing, all smart contracts carry inherent risk:
- Bugs in the core contracts could result in loss of funds.
- Bugs in the Uniswap V4 PoolManager or periphery contracts could affect pool operations.
- Oracle failures could lead to incorrect index prices and unexpected liquidations.

**Mitigation:** RLD's CDS product exists precisely to hedge this risk for other lending protocols. However, there is no CDS product for RLD itself.

---

## Collateral Risk

Your collateral is automatically wrapped into waUSDC (Aave V3 wrapped aUSDC). This means you have exposure to:
- **Aave V3 solvency** — if Aave accrues bad debt, waUSDC could depeg.
- **USDC stability** — if USDC depegs, your collateral value drops.
- **ERC-4626 wrapper risk** — the waUSDC wrapper is an additional smart contract layer.

---

## TWAMM Execution Risk

TWAMM streaming orders execute gradually over time. During the execution window:
- **Price can move against you** — your average execution price depends on market conditions throughout the entire duration.
- **Ghost settlement dependency** — your order's fill quality depends on taker flow, netting, and solver auctions. In low-activity periods, ghost inventory may accumulate without settlement.
- **Single-order limit** — Only one TWAMM order per margin account is tracked for NAV. If your streaming order loses value, your health factor drops.

---

## Limit Order Risk

Limit orders on RLD are **not tracked as collateral**. When you submit a limit order:
- The funds leave your margin account's NAV calculation.
- Your health factor drops immediately.
- If the order isn't filled, you can cancel to recover the funds.

This means aggressive limit order placement can inadvertently push your account toward liquidation, even if the order hasn't triggered yet.

---

## LP-Specific Risks

If you're providing liquidity:
- **Out-of-range risk:** Rates can stay outside your tick range longer than expected. While mean-reversion is structurally guaranteed, the timing is not.
- **Impermanent loss:** Temporary, but can be significant during extended rate dislocations.
- **Single-position tracking:** Only one LP position counts as collateral per margin account. Untracked positions are invisible to the solvency engine.

---

## Bond-Specific Risks

Synthetic bonds are **not risk-free fixed income.** Key risks:

- **Liquidation during extreme spikes:** If rates spike far beyond the initial hedge ratio, the margin account may become undercollateralized and get liquidated before the bond matures.
- **Basis risk:** The hedge is calibrated to the *expected* mean rate. Extreme, sustained deviations from the mean create tracking error between the short hedge and the floating yield.
- **TWAMM execution quality:** The buy-back order's average price depends on market conditions over the entire duration. Low liquidity periods may result in worse execution.
- **Freeze limitation:** While frozen, you cannot adjust positions. If the market moves dramatically, you're locked in until you unfreeze (which forfeits the fixed-rate guarantee).

## Summary

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Liquidation** | High | Maintain >1.5× health factor, don't over-leverage |
| **Funding decay** | Medium | Monitor premium/discount, avoid extended carry |
| **Smart contract** | Low (audited) | Diversify, don't deposit more than you can lose |
| **Collateral (Aave/USDC)** | Low | Systemic — limited mitigation |
| **TWAMM execution** | Medium | Use appropriate durations, monitor ghost settlement |
| **Limit order NAV gap** | Medium | Size limit orders carefully relative to margin |
| **LP out-of-range** | Medium | Use wider ranges, monitor rate movements |
| **Bond liquidation** | Low-Medium | Conservative sizing, monitor extreme rate scenarios |
